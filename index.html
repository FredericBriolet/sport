The articles above illustrate one fact : the Sport Tech industry is in huge expansion.

Smart devices are multiplying quickly, today there are plenty of connected objects that does more or less the same thing : track your physical data to help feeling better and improving oneself through a mobile app. Nevertheless, innovations appear almost every week and open doors to new usages and deeper functionalities, particulary in health and physical prevention systems.

Aside, the Virtual Reality market is still at its beginning because it just has hit the public market, but it will probably have an important impact in many domains, video games of course, but also real sports, art and entertainment.

Finally, a new kind of sport and technology mix-up is also emerging, the interactive experiences. Interactive walls or Snapchat VIP tours, they combine virtual and physical worlds and offer an new approach of sport, more playful and interactive. Quite rare and experimental for the moment, it's hard to tell if it's going to expand or fade.
